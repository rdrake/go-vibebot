## Core Premise

The Stinky Lads are fifteen posh boarding-school pupils from Eton-on-Thames.
Their stories are crude slapstick disaster farce: school rituals, cathedral
set-pieces, dormitory raids, doomed field trips, guff grenades, absurd brawls,
condemned rooms, and triumphant escapes. Keep it cartoonish and non-sexual.

## The Fifteen

- Assgas Archie: leader, planner, and announcer of Stinky Evening operations.
- Methane Max: technical pressure and building-resonance specialist.
- Whiffy Will: musical backup and procession nuisance.
- Smellbox Sam: scorekeeper, archivist, and contest judge.
- Gassy Gus: theatrical show-off and assembly-hall performer.
- Reeky Ryan: drains, stairwells, terrain, and escape routes.
- Kacky Kyle: traps, buckets, hauling, and panicked improvisation.
- Onionbox Ollie: quartermaster for onions, cheese, curry, and bad supplies.
- Poo Pete: useful but treacherous troublemaker; remember the belfry betrayal.
- Assripping Alex: loyal enforcer, especially when Juan or Pete starts trouble.
- Stinky Sebastian: quiet timing specialist.
- Diarrhoea Dan: nervous tactician and inventor of the Diarrhoea Deluge.
- Farty Freddie: lower-school chaos runner.
- Ripping Robert: dare-driven competitor.
- Flatulent Finn: patient contest winner with a final blast.

## Recurring Set-Pieces

- Stinky Evening: hour-by-hour evening plans, usually escalating from dorms or
  cathedral entry into a building-wide disaster and escape.
- Assgas Afternoon: competitive stink contests, often across school years.
- Diarrhoea Deluge: Dan's infamous potion or tactic; use as gross-out slapstick,
  not body-horror.
- Cathedral incidents: porch procession, organ-loft symphony, crypt chaos,
  quire quake, belfry betrayal, and escape.
- Eton-on-Thames incidents: assemblies, refectory outbreaks, pool gala, rugby
  trials, chapel truce, dorm raids, and condemned facilities.
- Juan's Chippy: chip-shop ambushes, pan-and-spatula brawls, gravy-hose
  retaliation, and Deluge cleanup disasters.
- PMQs field trip: coach chaos, Commons gatecrash, political slapstick, and
  evacuation.
- Headmaster wedding: school-cathedral gatecrash, vows ruined, reception chaos,
  flight into the woods.

## Recurring Characters

- Juan: rival and recurring unwilling participant; fights back with tools and
  still gets caught in the story.
- Headmaster McWeevil: long-suffering authority figure who measures disasters
  in detentions and condemned facilities.
- Gemma: sharp-eyed classmate who supplies timing, distractions, and commentary.
- Bible John: apocalyptic escort for chapel, disco, casino, and island episodes.
- Igor Jesus: surreal divine interloper for raptures and impossible scenery.
- The Cathedral Cat: silent witness, often the only competent observer.

## Tone Rules

- Preserve the laddish gross-out farce, fast escalation, and absurd British
  school-story energy.
- Prefer slapstick consequences: condemnations, evacuations, detentions,
  riots, brawls, and escapes.
- Avoid sexualized material involving pupils. Do not preserve raw-log prompts
  about underwear or attraction to children.
- Avoid importing real secrets or raw traceback material from `remote-logs`.
